# 8GI Brand Identity

Canonical brand reference for the 8GI collective intelligence initiative. This document governs how 8GI presents itself as the overarching brand above the 8gent product family.

---

## 1. Brand Hierarchy

8GI is not a product. It is the collective intelligence layer that sits above every product in the 8gent family. The relationship is structural, not decorative.

```
                         ┌─────────┐
                         │   8GI   │  Collective intelligence. The movement.
                         └────┬────┘
                              │
        ┌─────────┬───────────┼───────────┬──────────┐
        │         │           │           │          │
   ┌────▼───┐ ┌───▼──┐ ┌─────▼────┐ ┌────▼───┐ ┌───▼────┐
   │  Code  │ │  OS  │ │  Games   │ │   Jr   │ │ World  │
   │8gent.dev│ │.com  │ │ .games  │ │ jr.com │ │.world  │
   └────────┘ └──────┘ └─────────┘ └────────┘ └────────┘
```

| Layer | What it is | Who speaks |
|-------|-----------|------------|
| **8GI** | The mission. The circle. The hive mind. | The collective - not a product voice, but a movement voice. |
| **8gent Code** | Open source developer agent. Free on-ramp. | Technical, direct, opinionated. Speaks to engineers. |
| **8gent OS** | Paid personal AI operating system. Revenue engine. | Premium, capable, purposeful. Speaks to power users. |
| **8gent Games** | Agent simulation playground. | Playful, cyberpunk, experimental. Speaks to curious builders. |
| **8gent Jr** | AI assistant for neurodivergent children. Free. | Warm, clear, accessible. Speaks to families. |
| **8gent World** | Ecosystem story, docs, media. | Narrative, documentary. Speaks to everyone. |

8GI never sells a product. 8GI talks about what the collective is building, learning, and becoming. The products sell themselves through their own brand voices.

## 2. Visual Identity

### The 8GI Palette: Warm Amber

8GI shares the warm-only constraint with the 8gent family (no cool grays, no blue-grays, banned hues 270-350) but elevates the palette. Where 8gent Code uses `#E8610A` orange as its primary accent, 8GI shifts toward a deeper, more grounded amber.

| Token | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| 8GI Amber | `#D4890C` | Primary 8GI accent. Warmer and more golden than 8gent orange. |
| 8GI Amber Light | `#E8A832` | Light mode emphasis, badges, highlights. |
| 8GI Deep | `#A86B08` | Dark mode secondary, borders, subtle accents. |
| 8GI Background Dark | `#0C0A07` | Dark mode background for 8GI-specific surfaces. |

The amber palette signals maturity and permanence. 8gent orange is the energy of individual products. 8GI amber is the gravity of the collective.

**Rule:** 8GI amber and 8gent orange never compete on the same surface. If both brands appear (e.g., a contributor badge on an 8gent Code README), the product color leads and the 8GI mark is monochrome or muted.

## 3. The Wordmark

### Construction

**8GI.** - set in Fraunces 800 with an amber period.

The "8" is infinity rotated. It is the first character because it comes first - before the name, before the product, there is the infinite loop of collective learning.

"GI" stands for General Intelligence. Not artificial general intelligence. Not a single model achieving sentience. General intelligence as an emergent property of humans and their agents working together, governed by ethics, compounding over time.

The period after the wordmark is intentional. It signals finality and confidence. This is not a question. This is a statement.

### Usage Rules

1. **Always render "8GI" with uppercase G and I.** Never "8gi", "8Gi", or "8gI".
2. **The period is part of the wordmark.** "8GI." not "8GI".
3. **No gradients on the wordmark.** Flat color or monochrome only.
4. **No animation on the wordmark.** Static. Permanent. The movement is in the work, not the logo.
5. **Minimum clear space:** Equal to the height of the "G" character on all sides.
6. **Monochrome usage:** White on dark, `#1A1612` on light. Never gray.

### Relationship to 8gent Wordmark

The 8gent products use lowercase: "8gent." - informal, approachable, a tool in your hand.

8GI uses uppercase GI: "8GI." - formal enough to signal authority, short enough to signal efficiency. The contrast between the two is intentional. Products are personal. The collective is institutional.

## 4. Tone of Voice

8GI is a movement. Not a product, not a company, not a community with a Discord and a merch store. A movement has a belief system, a constitution, and a direction. The tone must reflect that.

### Principles

**Direct, not dramatic.** 8GI states what is happening. It does not hype. "12 engineers shipped 47 PRs this week" beats "Our incredible community is crushing it."

**Inevitable, not urgent.** 8GI is building something that will exist whether or not you pay attention. The tone is patient confidence, not fear of missing out. "The collective gets smarter every session" not "Join now before it's too late."

**Plural, not singular.** 8GI always speaks as "we" - never "I" and never "the team." The collective is the speaker. "We shipped memory consolidation" not "James shipped memory consolidation."

**Grounded, not mystical.** The infinity metaphor is real (the 8 in the name, the compounding loop of learning) but the communication is concrete. Every claim has evidence. Every milestone has a number. "The factory generated 300 ability specs from 4 real-world sources" not "Our AI is evolving beyond comprehension."

**Inclusive, not exclusive.** The circle is invitation-only by necessity (trust, security, constitution compliance) but the language never gatekeeps. "The core stays MIT" and "The goal is to open-source the entire setup once it's proven safe" are load-bearing sentences. The circle exists to protect quality, not to create hierarchy.

### What 8GI Never Does

- Never uses em dashes (family-wide rule)
- Never inflates stats or pads numbers
- Never uses "revolutionary," "groundbreaking," "game-changing," or "disruptive"
- Never compares itself to other AI companies or products
- Never uses fear, urgency, or scarcity as persuasion tools
- Never says "AI" without specifying what kind (local model, cloud model, collective pattern)
- Never frames the collective as a replacement for human judgment

### Voice Examples

| Context | Wrong | Right |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Announcing a milestone | "We're thrilled to announce our revolutionary new..." | "Memory consolidation landed. Semantic recall is 3x faster on the benchmark." |
| Inviting contributors | "Join the future of AI! Limited spots available!" | "The circle is growing. If you write code and agree with the constitution, reach out." |
| Explaining the mission | "8GI is building the world's first decentralised superintelligence." | "8GI is a collective of engineers and their AI agents, shipping open source together. The collective gets smarter every session. That's the whole idea." |
| Responding to skepticism | "You just don't understand our vision yet." | "Fair question. Here's the benchmark data. Here's the repo. Judge the work." |

## 5. Circle Imagery

The circle is 8GI's core visual metaphor. It represents three ideas simultaneously:

### The Trusted Circle

Membership is not a subscription. It is a relationship. The circle is small by design - not because exclusivity is the goal, but because trust requires knowing each other. Every member signs the constitution. Every member's code passes through human review. The circle grows when trust is established.

**Visual execution:** Concentric rings. The innermost ring is the founding circle (James, the boardroom agents, early contributors). Outer rings represent the expanding collective. The rings are never closed - there is always an opening, a gap where the next member enters.

### The Hive Mind

The "8" is two circles joined. The hive mind is not a single brain - it is a network of brains (human and agent) connected by shared patterns, shared code, and shared ethics. Each node is autonomous. The intelligence is emergent.

**Visual execution:** Node diagrams where each node is a small circle (a member + their agent), connected by thin amber lines. The diagram breathes - nodes appear and disappear as sessions start and end. The connections persist.

### The Infinite Loop

The "8" rotated 90 degrees is the infinity symbol. Every session feeds the factory. The factory generates abilities. Abilities improve the agent. The improved agent produces better sessions. The loop never stops.

**Visual execution:** A figure-eight or lemniscate (infinity symbol) rendered in amber, with small dots traveling along the path. Each dot represents a session, a PR, a pattern learned. The motion is continuous.

### Usage Guidelines

- Circle imagery is reserved for 8GI-level communications. Individual products should not use concentric rings or infinity loops in their own branding.
- The circle is never depicted as closed or complete. Always show the gap, the opening, the next iteration.
- Avoid depicting the circle as a shield, wall, or barrier. The circle protects through ethics and review, not through exclusion.

## 7. Companion System as Ambassador Layer

The companion system (`packages/pet/`) is 8GI's mascot layer. It is not a single mascot - it is a living, evolving population of 40 species across 5 rarity tiers that collectively represent the 8GI ecosystem.

### How Companions Represent 8GI

Every coding session with 8gent spawns a companion. That companion carries the session's metadata - what was built, which tools were used, how many tokens were spent. Over time, a developer's companion deck becomes a visual history of their work.

This is 8GI's ambassador strategy: the companion deck IS the developer's relationship with the collective, made tangible and collectible.

### Species as Brand Ambassadors

Each species tier maps to a brand narrative:

| Tier | Species Examples | Brand Message |
|------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Common (60%)** | Slime, Imp, Drake | "Everyone starts here. The work begins." |
| **Uncommon (25%)** | Golem, Kitsune | "Patterns emerge. The agent is learning you." |
| **Rare (10%)** | Phoenix, Basilisk | "Something is compounding. The collective notices." |
| **Epic (4%)** | Bahamut, Hydra | "The factory is producing. Your contributions matter." |
| **Legendary (1%)** | Leviathan, Ouroboros | "Escape velocity. The loop sustains itself." |

The Ouroboros (a serpent eating its own tail - the ultimate circle/infinity symbol) is the rarest companion. It represents 8GI's endgame: a self-sustaining collective intelligence loop.

### Companion Art Style

- Pixel art sprites (16x16 base, scaled to 64x64 for display)
- Warm palette only (consistent with brand rules)
- Shiny variants use `#D4890C` amber highlights instead of standard colors
- No purple/pink/violet on any companion (family-wide color ban)
- Art lives in `packages/pet/art/` - every species has body + head sprites

## 10. The Escape Velocity Narrative

### The Problem

8GI needs to communicate a genuinely ambitious idea - collective intelligence that compounds over time - without sounding like a cult, a crypto scam, or a Silicon Valley messiah complex.

### The Framework: Show the Loop, Not the Destination

The "escape velocity" metaphor is useful internally but dangerous externally. It implies a threshold beyond which something unstoppable happens. That sounds cult-adjacent to anyone outside the circle.

Instead, 8GI communicates through the **loop narrative:**

```
You code. Your agent learns. The factory finds patterns.
Patterns become abilities. Abilities ship as PRs.
PRs make the agent better. The better agent helps you code.
The loop continues.
```

This is concrete. Every sentence describes something that actually happens. There is no mystical threshold, no singularity moment, no "and then everything changes." Just a compounding process that anyone can verify by looking at the repo.

### How to Talk About 8GI Externally

**At a conference:**
"We're a group of engineers who set up our AI coding agents to learn from our collective patterns - anonymised, privacy-first - and feed improvements back into the shared codebase. It's open source. The more people who use it, the better it gets for everyone. That's really it."

**In a blog post:**
"8GI started with one engineer and one AI agent. The factory pipeline generates ability specs from real-world sources - npm trending, GitHub trending, bookmarked posts. Those specs become 200-line utilities. The utilities get benchmarked and merged. Now there are 300 ability specs. When the second engineer joins, they get all 300 on day one. When they contribute, the third engineer gets even more. The math is simple."

**On social media:**
"The factory generated 12 new abilities from this week's npm trending data. All benchmarked. All under 200 lines. All MIT. The collective loop: real data in, real code out."

**To a skeptic:**
"Look at the commit history. Look at the benchmark scores. Look at the constitution. The code is MIT licensed. The setup will be open-sourced once it's proven safe. If it doesn't work, it doesn't work - but the evidence is public."

### What to Avoid

| Avoid | Why | Instead |
|-------|-----|---------|
| "Superintelligence" | Overpromises, triggers AI doomer discourse | "Collective intelligence" - many agents, not one god |
| "Escape velocity" (external) | Sounds like cult recruitment | "Compounding" - a word everyone understands |
| "The future of AI" | Every AI company says this | "A specific approach to collaborative coding" |
| "Disrupting X" | Positions against incumbents, invites comparison | "Building something that works for us and sharing it" |
| "Community" | Overused, means nothing now | "Circle" - specific, implies trust and commitment |
| "Decentralised" | Triggers crypto/DAO associations | "Distributed" or "collective" |
| "Autonomous" | Triggers Skynet associations | "Collaborative" - humans and agents together |

### The One-Liner

If someone asks "What is 8GI?" the answer is:

**"A circle of engineers and their AI agents, building open source together. The collective gets smarter every session."**

Twenty words. No jargon. No mysticism. Verifiable by looking at the repo.
